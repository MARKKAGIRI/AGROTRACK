AGROTRACK GUIDE: TOMATO FARMING (KENYA)

==================================================
SECTION 1: VARIETY SELECTION
==================================================

DETERMINATE VARIETIES (Bush type - for open field):
- Cal J: Very popular, firm fruits, 70-80 days, good for market
- Marglobe: Disease resistant, medium-sized fruits, 75-85 days
- Roma VF: Processing type, elongated fruits, 75-80 days
- Money Maker: High yielding, round fruits, 70-75 days
- Rio Grande: Heat tolerant, paste-type, 80 days

INDETERMINATE VARIETIES (Climbing - for greenhouse):
- Anna F1: High yielding, disease resistant, 65-75 days
- Kilele F1: Premium quality, long shelf life, 70-80 days
- Prostar F1: Very high yielding, greenhouse variety
- Tylka F1: Bacterial wilt resistant, 75 days
- Eden Glory F1: Good for export, uniform fruits

CHERRY TOMATOES:
- Galilea F1: Sweet, high value, 60-70 days
- Cherry Gold: Yellow cherry, niche market

HYBRID vs OPEN POLLINATED:
- Hybrids (F1): Higher yield, disease resistant, expensive seed
- Open pollinated: Lower yield, can save seed, cheaper

==================================================
SECTION 2: SOIL & LAND PREPARATION
==================================================

SOIL REQUIREMENTS:
- Well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils
- pH: 6.0-6.8 (slightly acidic to neutral)
- Deep soils (60cm+) for good root development
- Rich in organic matter

SITE SELECTION:
- Full sunlight (at least 6-8 hours/day)
- Good drainage (avoid waterlogged areas)
- Protected from strong winds
- Access to water for irrigation

LAND PREPARATION:
- Deep ploughing to 30cm
- Incorporate 10-15 tons well-decomposed manure per acre
- Form raised beds: 1m wide, 15-20cm high
- Leave 50cm pathways between beds

==================================================
SECTION 3: NURSERY & TRANSPLANTING
==================================================

SEED SOWING:
- Nursery period: 3-4 weeks
- Sow in seed trays or nursery beds
- Use sterilized seedling mix (compost + soil + sand)
- Sowing depth: 1cm
- Seed rate: 200-300g per acre (open pollinated)
- Seed rate: 100-150g per acre (hybrid)

NURSERY MANAGEMENT:
- Water twice daily (morning and evening)
- Provide shade for first 7 days
- Gradually remove shade after germination
- Apply foliar feed at 2 weeks (e.g., Optimizer)
- Harden seedlings 1 week before transplanting

TRANSPLANTING:
- Seedling age: 3-4 weeks (4-6 true leaves)
- Transplant in evening or on cloudy day
- Spacing for determinate: 60cm × 45cm (12,000 plants/acre)
- Spacing for indeterminate: 90cm × 50cm (9,500 plants/acre)
- Water immediately after transplanting

==================================================
SECTION 4: FERTILIZER APPLICATION
==================================================

SOIL PREPARATION:
- Well-decomposed manure: 10-15 tons per acre
- Apply 2 weeks before transplanting

BASAL FERTILIZER (AT TRANSPLANTING):
- DAP: 200kg per acre (2 bags)
- Alternatively NPK 17:17:17: 200kg per acre
- Apply in planting holes, mix with soil

TOP DRESSING SCHEDULE:
- Week 3: CAN 50kg per acre
- Week 6: CAN 50kg per acre (during flowering)
- Week 9: NPK 20:10:10 50kg per acre (during fruiting)
- Week 12: CAN 50kg per acre
- Total CAN: 150kg; NPK: 50kg

FOLIAR FEEDING:
- Start at flowering stage
- Spray every 2 weeks until harvest
- Use balanced foliar fertilizers (Optimizer, Lavender)
- Include calcium and boron for better fruit quality
- Prevents blossom end rot

==================================================
SECTION 5: IRRIGATION & WATER MANAGEMENT
==================================================

WATER REQUIREMENTS:
- Tomatoes need consistent moisture
- Critical periods: Flowering and fruit development
- Avoid water stress (causes blossom end rot, fruit cracking)

IRRIGATION METHODS:
- Drip irrigation: Most efficient, reduces diseases
- Furrow irrigation: Common but promotes diseases
- Sprinkler: Not recommended (wets foliage, spreads diseases)

IRRIGATION SCHEDULE:
- Seedling stage: Light watering daily
- Vegetative stage: Every 2-3 days
- Flowering/fruiting: Every 2 days (more frequent in hot weather)
- Amount: 20-25mm per week

AVOID:
- Waterlogging (root diseases)
- Irregular watering (fruit cracking)
- Overhead irrigation (spreads leaf diseases)

==================================================
SECTION 6: STAKING & PRUNING
==================================================

STAKING (Indeterminate varieties):
- Use wooden or bamboo stakes (1.5-2m high)
- Install stakes at transplanting
- Tie plants loosely with soft twine
- For greenhouse: String support from overhead wires

PRUNING:
- Remove all suckers (side shoots from leaf axils)
- Keep only main stem for indeterminate varieties
- Prune weekly to maintain single stem
- Top the plant after 5-6 fruit trusses (greenhouses)
- Remove lower leaves touching ground

DETERMINATE VARIETIES:
- Minimal pruning required
- Remove only diseased or damaged leaves
- No staking needed (bush type)

==================================================
SECTION 7: PEST & DISEASE MANAGEMENT
==================================================

MAJOR PESTS:
- Whiteflies: Transmit viral diseases, suck sap
  Control: Yellow sticky traps, spray Imidacloprid or Acetamiprid
- Red Spider Mites: Bronzing of leaves, webbing
  Control: Spray Abamectin or Spiromesifen
- Tomato Leaf Miner (Tuta absoluta): Most serious pest, mines leaves and fruits
  Control: Pheromone traps, spray Chlorantraniliprole or Emamectin benzoate
- Aphids: Suck sap, transmit viruses
  Control: Spray Imidacloprid or Dimethoate
- African Bollworm: Bores into fruits
  Control: Spray Indoxacarb or Lambda-cyhalothrin

MAJOR DISEASES:
- Early Blight (Alternaria): Concentric rings on leaves
  Control: Spray Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil every 7-10 days
- Late Blight: Water-soaked lesions, white mold
  Control: Spray Metalaxyl + Mancozeb or Cymoxanil
- Bacterial Wilt: Sudden wilting, no yellowing
  Control: Use resistant varieties, crop rotation, remove infected plants
- Fusarium Wilt: Yellowing on one side, vascular browning
  Control: Use resistant varieties (F1 hybrids), soil fumigation
- Septoria Leaf Spot: Small brown spots with white centers
  Control: Spray copper fungicides or Chlorothalonil
- Blossom End Rot: Dark sunken spot on fruit base (calcium deficiency)
  Control: Maintain consistent moisture, foliar calcium sprays

VIRAL DISEASES:
- Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV): Yellowing, leaf curling, stunting
  Control: Control whiteflies, use resistant varieties, remove infected plants
- Tomato Mosaic Virus: Mottling, deformed leaves
  Control: Use certified seed, sanitize tools, remove infected plants

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM):
- Use yellow sticky traps for whiteflies and leaf miners
- Scout fields weekly
- Remove and destroy infected plants
- Rotate pesticides to avoid resistance
- Use biological controls where possible

SECTION 8: HARVESTING & POST-HARVEST
==================================================

HARVEST TIMING:
- Determinate: Start harvest 70-85 days after transplanting
- Indeterminate: Start harvest 65-75 days, continues for 2-3 months
- Harvest stage depends on market:
  * Green stage: For long-distance transport
  * Breaker stage: Light pink color starting
  * Pink stage: 30-60% pink color
  * Red ripe: Fully colored, for local market

HARVESTING PROCEDURE:
- Harvest early morning or late evening
- Pick with calyx (stem attached) for better shelf life
- Handle carefully to avoid bruising
- Use clean harvesting crates
- Harvest every 2-3 days during peak season

GRADING:
- Grade A: Large (>70mm), uniform, no defects
- Grade B: Medium (50-70mm), minor defects acceptable
- Grade C: Small (<50mm) or significant defects
- Reject diseased, cracked, or severely damaged fruits

POST-HARVEST HANDLING:
- Sort and grade immediately after harvest
- Pack in ventilated crates (do not overfill)
- Store in cool, shaded area (15-20°C ideal)
- For export: Pre-cool to 12-15°C within 6 hours
- Use refrigerated transport for long distances

SHELF LIFE:
- Green tomatoes: 2-3 weeks at 12-15°C
- Ripe tomatoes: 7-10 days at room temperature
- Do not refrigerate ripe tomatoes (affects flavor)

SECTION 9: YIELD & ECONOMICS
==================================================

EXPECTED YIELD:
- Open field (determinate): 15,000-25,000 kg per acre
- Greenhouse (indeterminate): 40,000-80,000 kg per acre
- Cherry tomatoes: 20,000-30,000 kg per acre

MARKET PRICES (Seasonal variation):
- Glut season: 20-40 KES per kg
- Off-season: 60-120 KES per kg
- Grade A premium: +30-50%
- Cherry tomatoes: 100-200 KES per kg

OPEN FIELD PRODUCTION COSTS PER ACRE:
- Seedlings: 15,000-25,000 KES
- Fertilizers: 30,000-40,000 KES
- Pesticides: 40,000-60,000 KES
- Labor: 50,000-80,000 KES
- Irrigation: 20,000-30,000 KES
- Other costs: 20,000 KES
- Total: 175,000-255,000 KES

INCOME POTENTIAL (Open field):
- 20,000 kg × 50 KES = 1,000,000 KES per acre
- Net profit: 700,000-800,000 KES per acre

GREENHOUSE PRODUCTION:
- Higher setup costs (500,000-2,000,000 KES)
- Production costs: 300,000-400,000 KES per acre
- Income: 2,000,000-4,000,000 KES per acre
- Net profit: 1,500,000-3,500,000 KES per acre

==================================================
SECTION 10: PROTECTED CULTIVATION
==================================================

GREENHOUSE TYPES:
- Simple polythene tunnel: 100,000-300,000 KES per acre
- Wooden greenhouse: 300,000-800,000 KES per acre
- Metallic greenhouse: 1,000,000-2,500,000 KES per acre

ADVANTAGES:
- Year-round production
- Higher yields (2-3x open field)
- Better quality fruits
- Reduced pest and disease pressure
- Premium prices

GREENHOUSE MANAGEMENT:
- Temperature: Keep below 30°C (ventilation critical)
- Humidity: 60-70% (prevent fungal diseases)
- Irrigation: Drip system essential
- Pruning: Single stem, regular de-suckering
- Pollination: May need hand pollination or bumblebees
