AGROTRACK GUIDE: POTATO FARMING (KENYA)

==================================================
SECTION 1: VARIETY SELECTION
==================================================

COMMON VARIETIES IN KENYA:
- Shangi: Most popular, high yielding, 90-100 days, oval tubers
- Dutch Robjin: Red skin, good for French fries, 100-110 days
- Kenya Mpya: White flesh, good keeper, 90-100 days
- Tigoni: Traditional variety, good for mashing, 120 days
- Sherekea: High yielding, disease tolerant, 90-100 days
- Unica: Good for chips, high dry matter, 100-110 days
- Purple/Markies: Export variety, high value

CERTIFIED SEED:
- Always use certified seed potatoes
- Avoid using ware potatoes as seed (low yield, diseases)
- Source from KEPHIS-certified producers
- Change seed every 2-3 seasons

==================================================
SECTION 2: SOIL & PLANTING
==================================================

SOIL REQUIREMENTS:
- Well-drained loamy or sandy loam soils
- pH: 5.0-6.5 (slightly acidic)
- Avoid heavy clay soils (poor drainage)
- Rich in organic matter

PLANTING TIME:
- Long rains: March-April (harvest July-August)
- Short rains: September-October (harvest January-February)
- High altitude areas: Can plant year-round with irrigation

LAND PREPARATION:
- Deep ploughing (30-40cm depth)
- Disk harrow to break clods
- Form ridges 75-90cm apart
- Remove all weeds and crop residues

SPACING & SEED RATE:
- Between rows: 75cm
- Within rows: 30cm
- Planting depth: 10-15cm
- Seed rate: 1,200-1,500kg per acre (certified seed)
- Plant size: 40-60g tubers preferred

PLANTING PROCEDURE:
- Cut large seed tubers (over 60g) into 2-3 pieces with 2-3 eyes each
- Allow cut surfaces to heal for 2 days
- Plant in ridges, eyes facing up
- Cover with 10-15cm soil

==================================================
SECTION 3: FERTILIZER APPLICATION
==================================================

BASAL FERTILIZER (AT PLANTING):
- DAP: 200kg per acre (2 bags)
- NPK 17:17:17: 200kg per acre (alternative)
- Mix with soil in planting furrow
- Can add 10 tons well-decomposed manure per acre

TOP DRESSING:
- CAN: 100kg per acre at 4-5 weeks after emergence
- Apply in bands 10cm from plants, then earth up
- Second top dressing: 50kg CAN at flowering (optional)

FOLIAR FEEDING:
- Apply at tuber formation stage
- Boosts yield and tuber quality
- Use NPK foliar fertilizers (e.g., Optimizer)

==================================================
SECTION 4: PEST & DISEASE MANAGEMENT
==================================================

MAJOR PESTS:
- Potato Tuber Moth: Larvae mine tubers and leaves
  Control: Earth up properly, harvest timely, spray with Profenofos
- Aphids: Transmit viral diseases
  Control: Spray with Dimethoate or Imidacloprid
- Cutworms: Cut seedlings at soil level
  Control: Apply Chlorpyrifos at planting
- Potato Cyst Nematode: Stunted growth, poor yields
  Control: Crop rotation, resistant varieties, soil fumigation

MAJOR DISEASES:
- Late Blight (Most serious): Brown lesions on leaves, tuber rot
  Control: Spray fungicides (Metalaxyl + Mancozeb) every 7-10 days
  Start at 3-4 weeks after emergence
- Early Blight: Concentric ring spots on older leaves
  Control: Spray with Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil
- Bacterial Wilt: Sudden wilting, brown vascular tissue
  Control: Use certified seed, crop rotation, remove infected plants
- Viral Diseases: Leaf roll, mosaic patterns
  Control: Use certified seed, control aphids, rogue out infected plants

SPRAY PROGRAM:
- Week 3-4: Start fungicide sprays (Mancozeb)
- Week 5-harvest: Alternate systemic and contact fungicides
- Spray interval: 7 days during wet weather, 10-14 days when dry
- Insecticide: Spray for aphids every 2-3 weeks

==================================================
SECTION 5: IRRIGATION & WATER MANAGEMENT
==================================================

WATER REQUIREMENTS:
- Potatoes need 500-700mm water during growing season
- Critical periods: Tuber initiation and bulking (50-80 days)

IRRIGATION SCHEDULING:
- Irrigate when soil moisture drops to 65-70% of field capacity
- Frequency: Every 5-7 days during dry periods
- Amount: 25-30mm per irrigation

METHODS:
- Furrow irrigation (most common)
- Drip irrigation (best efficiency, expensive)
- Sprinkler irrigation (moderate)

AVOID:
- Waterlogging (causes tuber rots)
- Moisture stress during tuber formation (reduces yield)
- Irrigation 2 weeks before harvest (improves storage)

==================================================
SECTION 6: WEED CONTROL & EARTHING UP
==================================================

WEED CONTROL:
- Pre-emergence herbicide: Apply 2-3 days after planting
  (e.g., Metribuzin at 0.5kg/acre)
- Hand weeding: 3-4 weeks after planting
- Second weeding combined with earthing up at 6-8 weeks

EARTHING UP (CRITICAL):
- First earthing up: 4-5 weeks after emergence
- Second earthing up: 6-8 weeks after emergence
- Pull soil to ridge height of 25-30cm
- Prevents tuber greening
- Improves drainage
- Controls tuber moth

==================================================
SECTION 7: HARVESTING & POST-HARVEST
==================================================

HARVEST TIMING:
- Early varieties: 90-100 days after planting
- Late varieties: 110-120 days
- Physiological maturity: When vines turn yellow and die
- Market demand: Can harvest early for higher prices

MATURITY INDICATORS:
- Vines yellowing and dying back naturally
- Skin firmly attached to tuber (rub test)
- Adequate tuber size for market

HARVESTING PROCEDURE:
- Choose dry weather
- Cut vines 10-14 days before digging (skin hardening)
- Dig carefully to avoid cuts and bruises
- Avoid exposing tubers to direct sunlight (greening)
- Sort and grade immediately

GRADING:
- Grade 1 (large): >60mm diameter - high price
- Grade 2 (medium): 40-60mm - moderate price
- Grade 3 (small/chats): <40mm - low price or processing
- Reject diseased, damaged, and green tubers

CURING:
- Store freshly harvested potatoes at 12-15°C
- High humidity (85-90%) for 10-14 days
- Allows skin to heal minor wounds
- Improves storage life

STORAGE:
- Cool, dark, well-ventilated stores
- Temperature: 4-8°C (ambient in highland areas)
- Humidity: 85-90%
- Avoid light (causes greening and solanine production)
- Remove sprouted tubers regularly
- Storage life: 2-4 months at ambient, 6+ months in cold storage

SECTION 8: YIELD & ECONOMICS
==================================================

EXPECTED YIELD:
- Good management: 200-300 bags per acre (110kg bags)
- Average: 120-180 bags per acre
- Poor management: 60-100 bags per acre

MARKET PRICES (Seasonal variation):
- Harvest season glut: 1,500-2,500 KES per bag
- Off-season: 3,000-5,000 KES per bag
- Grade 1 premium: +30-50% above average

INCOME POTENTIAL:
- 200 bags × 2,500 KES = 500,000 KES per acre (harvest time)
- 150 bags × 4,000 KES = 600,000 KES per acre (stored until off-season)

PRODUCTION COSTS PER ACRE:
- Certified seed: 60,000-90,000 KES
- Fertilizers: 30,000-40,000 KES
- Pesticides: 25,000-35,000 KES
- Labor: 40,000-60,000 KES
- Other costs: 20,000 KES
- Total: 175,000-245,000 KES

NET PROFIT:
- Harvest time sale: 250,000-300,000 KES per acre
- Off-season sale: 350,000-400,000 KES per acre

==================================================
SECTION 9: CROP ROTATION & SOIL HEALTH
==================================================

ROTATION IMPORTANCE:
- Potatoes are heavy feeders
- Continuous cropping depletes soil
- Builds up pests and diseases (especially bacterial wilt)

RECOMMENDED ROTATION:
- Year 1: Potatoes
- Year 2: Cereals (maize, wheat)
- Year 3: Legumes (beans, peas)
- Year 4: Return to potatoes

BREAK CROPS:
- Oats, wheat, or barley (reduce bacterial wilt)
- Cruciferous crops like kale (biofumigation)

SOIL IMPROVEMENT:
- Add 10-20 tons manure per acre annually
- Green manure crops in rotation
- Maintain soil organic matter >3%

==================================================
SECTION 10: VALUE ADDITION
==================================================

PROCESSING OPPORTUNITIES:
- Potato crisps/chips (highest value)
- French fries (frozen or fresh-cut)
- Dehydrated potato products
- Seed potato production (requires certification)

MARKETS:
- Fresh market (supermarkets, greengrocers)
- Processing companies (Kevian, Frigoken)
- Hotels and restaurants
- Export (grade 1 ware and seed potatoes)

CONTRACT FARMING:
- Sign agreements with processors
- Guaranteed market and price
- Technical support provided
- Specific varieties required
